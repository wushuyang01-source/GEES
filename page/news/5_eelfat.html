GEES Electric Pilot Boat Control System Successfully Passes Factory Acceptance Test

13-meter pure electric pilot boat GCS control system completes comprehensive FAT at Zenergy facility in Changshu, Jiangsu, supporting Indonesia export project

By GEES Engineering Team 4 min read
General Energies' 13-meter pure electric pilot boat GCS control system undergoing Factory Acceptance Test at Zenergy facility in Changshu, Jiangsu

Successful Factory Acceptance Test Completion

Changshu, Jiangsu, July 12, 2024 — The latest 13-meter pure electric pilot boat GCS control system developed by General Energies successfully completed its Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) at Zenergy's facility in Changshu, Jiangsu.

The comprehensive testing, which began on July 7, 2024, and concluded on July 12, 2024, passed all requirements, providing robust technical support for the shipyard exporting to Indonesia and marking a significant milestone in electric marine propulsion technology.

Comprehensive Testing System Components

The entire testing system showcased a sophisticated integration of cutting-edge technologies, demonstrating the advanced capabilities of General Energies' electric propulsion solutions.

Advanced Battery Technology

The testing system featured liquid-cooled battery packs jointly developed by Zenergy and General Energies, each pack with a capacity of 195 kWh, totaling two packs for a combined 390 kWh capacity.

  • High Capacity Design: 390 kWh total capacity providing extended operational range for demanding pilot operations
  • Liquid Cooling Technology: Advanced thermal management system ensuring optimal performance in tropical climates
  • Joint Development: Collaborative innovation between Zenergy and General Energies for enhanced reliability
  • Modular Configuration: Two 195 kWh packs allowing for flexible power management and redundancy
  • Performance Validation: Comprehensive testing confirming all technical specifications and safety standards

This test was crucial for all parties involved. By passing the FAT, the time required for on-board commissioning was significantly reduced, marking an unprecedented milestone with immense value for the project and future electric vessel deployments.
