Silver Raven: UK's First Electric Cruise Vessel

GEES with with Vulkan Hybrid to Powers UK's Revolutionary Zero-Emission Tourist Vessel for Tower of London River Tours

By GEES Engineering Team 6 min read
The Silver Reven - UK's first electric cruise vessel under construction at Pendennis Shipyard, Cornwall

Historic Partnership for Sustainable Tourism

A generations-old Woods family business is making history by supporting British shipbuilding to lead the world in sustainable luxury tourism. Woods' Silver Fleet has announced their first zero-emission tourist vessel, the Silver Reven, powered by General Energies Electric Ship (GEES) technology and scheduled to launch summer 2025.

The 39-meter vessel will serve as the official Tower of London River Tour in partnership with Historic Royal Palaces, offering sustainable tourism on one of the world's most iconic waterways.

GEES Electric Propulsion System

The Silver Raven features GEES's comprehensive General Control System (GCS), representing a collaboration between GEES and Vulkan Italy. The system includes:

Propulsion Motors

2 x 165kW liquid-cooled electric motors

Battery System

2 x 360 kWh EST Floattech Octopus Series

Fast Charging

4 x 250kW DC + 2 x 44kW AC charging

Emergency Power

2 x 125kW generator sets

Hotel Load

2 x 50kVA transformers

Smart Systems

Energy Management System (EMS) & Propulsion Control System (PCS)

Innovative Design & Construction

Built by the renowned Pendennis Shipyard in Cornwall, the Silver Reven represents cutting-edge maritime technology:

  • Ultra-Lightweight Construction: Advanced aluminum design reducing weight while maintaining strength
  • Rapid Charging: 2-megawatt shore charging facility using sustainable energy sources
  • Heritage Design: Clean, contemporary lines echoing 1920s-30s Thames cruising era
  • Premium Materials: Bespoke wool carpet from Kidderminster looms and Scottish leather seating
  • Modern Accessibility: Full 21st-century accessibility features throughout

Environmental Leadership

The Silver Reven sets new standards for sustainable maritime tourism:

  • Zero Emissions: First zero-emission tourist vessel in the UK
  • Clean Maritime Innovation: Part of UK's Clean Maritime Demonstration Competition Round 4 (CMDC4)
  • Government Support: Funded by Department for Transport through £206m UK Shore program
  • Ecosystem Protection: No risk of fuel spillage in the Thames
  • Noise Reduction: Whisper-quiet operation enhancing passenger experience

Industry Impact & Future

Toby Allies, Managing Director at Pendennis, commented: "The start of the Silver Reven project has been an exciting period for the yard and all the team involved. The aluminum construction specified on the project is all in-house providing a perfect backdrop for some of our more experienced team members to transfer their skills and knowledge to a whole new generation of fabricators."

This groundbreaking project supports maritime apprenticeships in Falmouth and demonstrates how traditional British shipbuilding can lead global innovation in sustainable marine technology.

About the Partnership

The Silver Reven represents a unique collaboration between traditional British maritime heritage and cutting-edge Chinese electric technology. GEES's partnership with Vulkan Italy and Woods' Silver Fleet demonstrates how international cooperation can drive sustainable innovation in the maritime industry.

As part of the UK government's commitment to decarbonizing domestic maritime transport, this project showcases how British shipbuilding can maintain its world-leading position while embracing environmental responsibility.
