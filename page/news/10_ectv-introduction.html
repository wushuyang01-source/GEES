The GEES E-CTV WindGuard Series - Advanced electric crew transfer vessel designed for offshore wind operations

Revolutionizing Offshore Wind Support

General Energies is proud to introduce the E-CTV (Electric Crew Transfer Vessel) WindGuard Series, a groundbreaking advancement in sustainable offshore wind support vessels. Designed specifically for crew transfer operations in offshore wind farms, these vessels represent the future of zero-emission maritime support.

The WindGuard Series combines cutting-edge electric propulsion technology with proven marine engineering to deliver unmatched performance, reliability, and environmental responsibility in challenging offshore conditions.

Offshore Wind Industry Benefits

  • Zero Emissions: Completely electric operation eliminates diesel emissions in sensitive marine environments
  • Reduced Operating Costs: Lower fuel and maintenance costs compared to conventional diesel vessels
  • Silent Operation: Minimal noise impact on marine life and offshore working conditions
  • Weather Resilience: Advanced hull design and stability systems for safe operations in challenging conditions
  • Crew Comfort: Vibration-free electric operation improves crew comfort during long transfers
  • Reliability: Fewer moving parts and simplified drivetrain reduce maintenance requirements

Market Impact and Sustainability

The E-CTV WindGuard Series addresses the offshore wind industry's growing demand for sustainable support vessels. As offshore wind farms expand globally, the need for environmentally responsible crew transfer solutions becomes increasingly critical.

By eliminating diesel emissions and reducing operational noise, the WindGuard Series supports the offshore wind industry's commitment to environmental stewardship while delivering superior operational performance.

Global Applications

The WindGuard Series is designed for worldwide deployment in various offshore wind markets:

  • European Waters: North Sea, Baltic Sea, and Atlantic offshore wind farms
  • Asian Markets: Taiwan Strait, East China Sea, and Japanese offshore developments
  • Americas: US East Coast and emerging South American offshore wind projects
  • Emerging Markets: Support for new offshore wind developments globally
